Pad Thai Recipe

Description

Pad Thai is a popular Thai dish consisting of stir-fried rice noodles, eggs, tofu or shrimp, bean sprouts, and various vegetables, flavored with a combination of tamarind paste, fish sauce, garlic, chili peppers, and palm sugar. It is often garnished with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and cilantro. Pad Thai is known for its sweet, sour, and savory flavors, as well as its vibrant colors and textures. It is a staple street food in Thailand and has gained popularity worldwide for its delicious taste and versatility.

Ingredients Needed:

  • Rice noodles
  • Eggs
  • Tofu or shrimp (choose one)
  • Bean sprouts
  • Garlic

Steps to cook:

  1. Soak rice noodles in hot water until softened, then drain. Beat eggs in a bowl. Cut tofu into cubes or peel and devein shrimp. Mince garlic.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add tofu or shrimp and cook until golden brown or cooked through. Remove from skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add a bit more oil if needed. Add minced garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Add soaked rice noodles and stir-fry for a few minutes until heated through.
  4. Push noodles to one side of the skillet and pour beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble eggs until cooked, then mix with the noodles. Add tofu or cooked shrimp back to the skillet. Pour Pad Thai sauce (made with tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, and chili peppers) over the noodles and toss everything together until well combined.
  5. Add bean sprouts and toss briefly until slightly wilted. Remove from heat and transfer Pad Thai to serving plates. Garnish with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and cilantro. Serve hot and enjoy!
